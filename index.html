Our Courses

Interactive Sessions

Private Pilot License

The Private Pilot license is the first certification aspiring pilots must accomplish. With the private pilot's license, the pilot may fly anywhere under visual meteorological conditions (VMC). A pilot can also fly passengers for nonprofit, and have access to any single engine aircraft with the appropriate ratings.

Interactive Sessions

Instrument Rating

The Instrument Rating enables a certified pilot to fly by instruments and through instrument meteorological conditions (IMC). The instrument rating is a great benefit to all pilots in case of weather restrictions. The instrument rating opens doors to new challenges in your aviation adventure.

Interactive Sessions

Multi-Engine Rating

The Multi Engine Rating allows a certified pilot to fly a multi engine airplane. Pilots considering the airline industry should consider this rating, as many companies will consider flight experience on an airplane with two engines valuable.

Interactive Sessions

Commercial Pilot License

The Commercial License is an important license to pilots who are considering flying for profit. This will include training in complex aircraft that will meet the requirements of earning the license. With this license in hand, a pilot finally has the option to fly for hire.

Interactive Sessions

Flight Instructor License

The Flight Instructor license will allow a pilot to instruct and teach the future generations of student pilots. Teaching aviation has great benefits as well as help the pilot become a better aviator.

Interactive Sessions

Airline Transport Pilot License (ATP)

The Airline Transport Pilot License (ATP) is the highest pilot rating. It will enable the applicant to fly as an airline transport pilot or an aircraft weighing more than 12,500 pounds. Fly the California Airways with confidence. The ATP is the most important license requirement for an airline career job.

Immerse yourself in a large collaborative environment.

Dream Big, a non-profit organization started small as in-person group of 20 students and parents in a sunroom with a primary mission to educate our community about the college admission process. Today, this has grown to a larger group with over 819 members and six high-school volunteers harnessing the power of social media. In this collaborative environment, parents and students can get their questions clarified by communicating with the engaging group members. Other free services we provide include panel discussions with high school and college students, a breakdown of the college admissions process, informational sessions with reputable college counselors, and valuable sessions from current & former college admissions officers.
